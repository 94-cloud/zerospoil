## FALSE_POSITIVES:

The detection rule triggering on all svchost-from-services.exe relationships is overly broad and creates significant noise.

- **Null ExecutablePath on legitimate svchost instances:** The WMI `ExecutablePath` field being null for system32-resident svchost processes is a known WMI behavior on Windows 10/11 with PPL-protected processes. The `cmdline` field (populated via a different mechanism) correctly reflects their identity. These are **low-confidence anomalies on their own** and should not be treated as independent findings without corroborating evidence.

- **services.exe and lsass.exe null paths (PIDs 816, 836):** Likely a legitimate data collection limitation for early-boot protected processes. **Insufficient evidence alone** to conclude compromise — however they remain flagged at HIGH due to the active compromise context.
